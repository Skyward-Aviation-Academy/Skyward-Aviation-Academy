Instrument Flight Rules (IFR)

Overview

The IFR training program enhances your ability to fly solely by reference to instruments, allowing you to operate in various weather conditions.

Requirements

To enroll in the IFR program, students must already hold a Private Pilot License (PPL) and meet the necessary medical and proficiency requirements.

What You'll Learn

Training includes advanced navigation techniques, instrument approaches, weather interpretation, and air traffic control communications.

Flight Hours

A minimum of 40 hours of instrument flight training is required, including both simulated and actual instrument flight conditions.
